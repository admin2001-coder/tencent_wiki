1) Global web & app acceleration (CDN + smart routing)

Improve time-to-first-byte and page load by serving content from edge nodes and optimizing routes. Commonly used when expanding globally or serving mixed static + dynamic workloads.

  • Multi-region websites and SaaS dashboards
  • Large file downloads and software distribution
  • Video / live streaming delivery at scale
  • Traffic surges (launches, campaigns)
CDNSmart accelerationGlobal delivery

2) Web protection (WAF) for internet-facing apps

Shield applications from common OWASP-style attacks and abusive traffic patterns. This is often paired with acceleration so security controls sit at the edge.

  • Protect login, checkout, and search endpoints
  • Block exploits, injection, and malicious scanning
  • Enforce policies before traffic reaches origin
WAFEdge securityApp shielding

3) DDoS mitigation to sustain availability

Maintain uptime during volumetric attacks and sudden bursts. Organizations often adopt DDoS protection when availability is revenue-critical.

  • E-commerce: keep checkout and payment stable
  • Gaming: protect game login/download services during launches
  • Media: absorb traffic floods during major events
DDoSAvailabilityBurst handling

4) Bot management for fraud & abuse reduction

Identify and mitigate automated traffic that impacts cost, inventory, fraud, scraping, and account takeover risks.

  • Stop credential stuffing and automated signups
  • Reduce scraping and inventory hoarding
  • Protect APIs from abnormal request patterns
Bot managementFraud controlsAPI protection

Reference architecture (high level)

  • Users → Edge nodes for acceleration (caching, routing) and security inspection (WAF/DDoS/bot).
  • Clean traffic proceeds to your origin (cloud load balancer, Kubernetes, VM, or serverless backend).
  • Observed metrics drive tuning: caching rules, security policies, rate limits, and bot challenges.

This page intentionally keeps architecture vendor-neutral while linking to official EdgeOne product pages and docs for details.
